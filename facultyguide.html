Faculty Guide: Graphic Syllabus Template 26F.1

This guide is designed to help you efficiently complete your course syllabus using the 26F.1 Graphic Template. This version prioritizes clarity for students and reduces administrative maintenance for faculty by utilizing the Syllabus Information Page (SIP).

Important Template Note:

All information within the Word document that must be addressed or customized for your specific course is highlighted in yellow.

1. Administrative Workflow & Compliance

  • Chair/Dean Review: A copy of your completed syllabus must be provided to your Department Chair or Dean for review before the start of the semester.
  • Syllabus Repository: Once approved, faculty are required to upload their final syllabus to the institutional SharePoint repository: [Link to SharePoint Site Placeholder]
  • Moodle Activation: All courses must have a current PDF syllabus uploaded to an active Moodle course site by the Friday before the course start date.
  • Final Format: Always export your final document as a screen-reader-friendly PDF. This prevents unauthorized altering of content and ensures ADA compliance.

2. Populating Course Assignments & Schedule

The template provides flexibility in how you manage your data:

Entry Methods

  • Option A: Coursetrix Integration (Recommended): Build your schedule at https://coursetrix.com/. Copy and paste the automated "Assignment Type" and "Course Schedule" tables directly into Pages 9â€“11.
  • Option B: Manual Entry: Manually enter data into the provided table structures. Ensure your total points align with your specific course grading scale.

Best Practices

  • Eliminate Placeholders: Ensure all bracketed [Text] and yellow-highlighted placeholders are removed or replaced before distribution.
  • Keep Descriptions Brief: High-level categories (e.g., "Research Essay") are sufficient for the syllabus. Detailed instructions and rubrics should reside in Moodle.
  • Link to Outcomes: Every assignment in your schedule table must include its corresponding Course Level Learning Outcome (e.g., CLLO: 1).
  • "Week Zero": Consider using a "Week Zero" module in Moodle to provide software tutorials and tech-check assignments. This ensures students are prepared before the academic content begins.

3. Course-Specific Policies

There are two types of customization required in this section:

A. Free-Form Policies (Drafting Tips)

These require specific descriptive text. Use these strategies for clarity:

  • Attendance & Participation:

    Define what "active participation" looks like for your modality. For online courses, clarify that logins are not attendance; participation in weekly activities (quizzes/discussions) is. Be explicit about point deductions to prevent end-of-term disputes.

  • Makeup Exam Policy:

    Balance rigor with empathy. Specify a notification window (e.g., 24 hours prior to the exam) and list acceptable documentation. Avoid "no makeups under any circumstances" to remain aligned with ADA and Title IX requirements.

  • Grading Timeframes:

    Under-promise and over-deliver. If you promise feedback within 7 days, stick to it. Setting a clear expectation here significantly reduces the volume of "When will this be graded?" emails.

  • Proctored Exams:

    List specific hardware (webcam, RAM) and environment requirements. Recommend a "Practice Proctoring Quiz" early in the term to identify technical issues before high-stakes assessments.

B. Data & Information Entry

These are simple entries for dates, locations, or logistical facts (highlighted in yellow):

  • Census Dates: Specific deadlines for attendance verification.
  • Final Exam/Activity: Exact date and time of the final assessment.
  • Course Format: Modality, meeting times, and physical or virtual locations.
  • Contact Info: Faculty email, office hours, and response times.

4. Managing the Generative AI (GenAI) Policy

The Foundational vs. Scalable Skills framework is a standardized institutional policy.

  • Mandatory Framework: Do not delete or alter the definitions of Foundational and Scalable skills on Page 4.
  • Customization: You may leave the standard policy paragraph as it is, or add to it with your own course-specific considerations. The following items are provided as examples of how you might expand that section:

  • Example Addition (Foundational Focus):

    "This course focuses on Foundational Skills; therefore, the use of GenAI is not permitted for any assignment or phase of work."

  • Example Addition (Scalable/Advanced Focus):

    "For this advanced course, you will focus on applying your foundational knowledge and using AI to amplify your skills. Refer to the specific assignment instructions to guide your appropriate use of GenAI in this course."

5. The Syllabus Information Page (SIP)

The template off-loads high-maintenance policies to www.cochise.edu/SIP.

  • Automatic Updates: You no longer need to manually update text for ADA, Title IX, FERPA, or general Safety Procedures.
  • The "Hidden Curriculum": The SIP provides students with deeper explanations of academic terms like "Office Hours" and "Census Dates," allowing your syllabus to remain a concise, visual guide.
